# Threadline — Product Vision

> Threadline turns consumer frustration into product briefs before a brand runs a focus group.

---

## The Problem

The adaptive fashion market is underserved and growing — but the brands operating in it are largely guessing what to build.

There is no consumer intelligence tool built for this space. Brands like Tommy Hilfiger Adaptive, Silverts, and Reboundwear make product decisions based on internal assumptions, occasional surveys, or expensive, slow focus groups. Meanwhile, consumers with post-mastectomy needs, ostomy conditions, rheumatoid arthritis, and post-surgical recovery are posting detailed, specific, emotionally honest accounts of what doesn't work and what they wish existed — on Reddit, in Amazon reviews, in community forums.

That signal exists. No one is systematically reading it.

Focus groups are slow. They are expensive. They are scheduled. By the time results come back, months have passed. And they still depend on someone in a room asking the right questions.

Threadline replaces that process with something faster, cheaper, and grounded in what consumers are already saying unprompted.

---

## The Solution

Threadline is a web application that continuously collects consumer posts and reviews from Reddit and Amazon, extracts pain points and product feature signals using AI, and surfaces them as pre-generated ranked product opportunities and detailed product briefs — available instantly, for any of the four conditions it covers at launch.

A product manager opens Threadline, selects a condition, and sees:

- The top unmet needs for that condition, ranked by signal strength
- Ready-to-act product ideas generated from those signals
- A full product brief for any idea — confirmed pain points, recommended materials, closures, sizing, and what to build first
- Cross-condition overlap flagged automatically when the same need appears across multiple conditions

No pre-formed idea required. No focus group. No waiting.

---

## Who It's For

Threadline is built for anyone making product decisions in adaptive fashion:

- **Product managers at established adaptive fashion brands** — Tommy Hilfiger Adaptive, Silverts, Reboundwear, and others — who need to know what to build next before committing to a development cycle
- **Founders and startups** entering adaptive fashion for the first time, who need to validate whether a market opportunity is real before investing in it
- **Design researchers and strategists** at consultancies working with brands in this space

The primary user is a product manager — someone who is time-poor, needs actionable output, and has the authority to act on what Threadline surfaces.

---

## What Makes Threadline Different

There is no other product intelligence tool built specifically for adaptive fashion. The broader market research space has general-purpose tools, but none that:

- Are built on data from the specific communities where adaptive fashion consumers speak honestly
- Surface cross-condition overlap as a signal (a feature need shared across three conditions is a larger market opportunity than one shared by one)
- Go from raw consumer language to a specific, actionable product brief automatically
- Update continuously from live data rather than from a one-time study
- Allow PMs to talk with the data — asking questions and getting answers grounded in real consumer signals (Phase 2)

Threadline is not a report. It is not a dashboard of generic trends. It is a tool that tells a brand PM exactly what to build, for whom, and why — backed by the words of real consumers.

---

## Conditions Covered at Launch

| Condition | Why included |
|---|---|
| Post-mastectomy / breast cancer recovery | High volume of consumer conversation in dedicated communities; strong unmet needs around clothing and comfort post-surgery |
| Ostomy | Active online communities; specific garment needs that mainstream fashion does not address |
| Rheumatoid arthritis / mobility limitations | Large and growing population; significant frustration with standard clothing that does not accommodate mobility limitations |
| Post-surgical recovery (general) | Broad condition with overlapping needs with the above three; active consumer conversation on Reddit and Amazon |

## Business Model

| Stage | Model | Details |
|---|---|---|
| Beta | Free | Open access to establish trust, gather feedback, and build the data foundation |
| Growth | Paid reports | Brands pay per detailed product brief or condition report |
| Scale | Subscription | Monthly subscription for ongoing access to the full intelligence platform |

---

## What Threadline Is Not

- It is not a social listening tool for general fashion
- It is not a competitor tracker
- It is not a survey platform
- It is not a report that gets delivered once and goes stale

Threadline is a living intelligence platform. The data updates weekly. The briefs reflect what consumers are saying now, not what they said six months ago in a focus group.
